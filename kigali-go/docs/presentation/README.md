## ğŸ¯ **Quick Start Guide (NEW - Use This!)**

### **Step 1: Team Meeting - Read Together** (Day 1, 2 hours)
1. **All members read:** `PRESENTATION_SUMMARY.md` (overview)
2. **All members read:** `TECHNICAL_PRESENTATION_GUIDE.md` (main guide)
3. **Assign roles:** 6 members, each gets 3-4 slides
   - Member 1: Project Overview & Problem (Slides 1-3)
   - Member 2: Features & Technology Stack (Slides 4-7)
   - Member 3: Database & Data Management (Slides 8-11)
   - Member 4: System Architecture (Slides 12-15)
   - Member 5: Code Quality & Challenges (Slides 16-19)
   - Member 6: Feedback & Next Steps (Slides 20-24)

### **Step 2: Individual Work - Create Your Slides** (Days 2-3, 3 hours each)
1. **Reference:** `SLIDE_DATA_POINTS.md` for your section's data
2. **Create:** Your 3-4 slides in PowerPoint/Google Slides
3. **Follow:** Design guidelines (colors, fonts, layout)
4. **Include:** All required data points and visuals
5. **Write:** Speaker notes for your section

### **Step 3: Team Review & Refinement** (Day 4, 2 hours)
1. **Share:** All slides with team for feedback
2. **Ensure:** Consistent design across all 25 slides
3. **Verify:** All data is accurate and complete
4. **Practice:** Transitions between speakers

### **Step 4: Rehearsal Week** (Days 5-7, 1 hour daily)
- **Day 5:** First full run-through with timer (aim for 7 minutes)
- **Day 6:** Second rehearsal, refine timing and transitions
- **Day 7:** Final rehearsal, Q&A practice

### **Step 5: Teamwork Report** (Days 5-7, parallel work)
1. **Use:** `TEAMWORK_REPORT_TEMPLATE.md`
2. **Gather:** All 23 required screenshots
3. **Document:** Individual contributions
4. **Compile:** Into professional PDF

### **Step 6: Presentation Day**
1. **Print:** `QUICK_REFERENCE_GUIDE.md` for each member
2. **Arrive:** 30 minutes early
3. **Test:** Equipment and backup files
4. **Present:** Confidently (7 min + 3 min Q&A)
5. **Submit:** Both presentation slides and teamwork report PDF

## ğŸ¤ **Speaking Tips**

### **For All Members:**
1. **Speak clearly and pace yourself** - Don't rush
2. **Make eye contact** with camera
3. **Use hand gestures** naturally (but not excessively)
4. **Show enthusiasm** - You believe in this project
5. **Support teammates** - Nod, smile when others speak
6. **Watch the clock** - Each member is responsible for their time

### **Transition Phrases:**
- "Now, let's look at..."
- "Building on that point..."
- "This leads us to..."
- "Let me hand it over to [Name]..."

### **If You Get Stuck:**
- Pause, breathe, reframe
- "Let me clarify that..."
- "To put it another way..."
- Team support: Others can jump in
